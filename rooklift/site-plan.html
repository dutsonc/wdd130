Overview

Purpose

The purpose of Rook Lift is to educate beginner chess players directly, and to provide oppourtunity for further learning through coaching. This site will provide information on chess principles, and will include visual depictions of games to allow for more accesible knowledge. This site will also link visitors to important information which can be used to schedule 1 on 1, and group lessons.

Audience

The target audience is anybody who is seeking to learn more about chess, whether through online lessons or through direct lessons. The audience will consist of people who are introduced to the site for private lessons, or anybody older than about 8 years old, who wants to learn more about chess in general, such as somebody researching the openings the site covers.

Queen's Gambit Introduction

One of the most popular openings in Chess, and the title of a popular Netflix series, the Queen's Gambit is a must-have weapon in any chess players arsenal. As its name suggests, this opening is a gambit, meaning that while it involves potentially sacrificing some material early on, it can often lead to significant advantages as the game progresses. While not the most secure opening, the Queen's Gambit (QG) can provide the player with the white pieces with early advantages, sharp traps, and attacking opportunities in just a few moves. To begin the game, white plays the move pawn to d4. This is the second most popular first move for white and is often referred to as the Queen's Pawn Opening. This initial move puts a pawn in the center of the board, and immediately protects the e5 square, and the c5 square. Without getting into the weeds, this move is one of the two most sensible ways to begin. Following white's move, black plays pawn to d5, which prevents white from playing pawn to e4 on their second move, contesting the center, and creating space in front of the queen. In response to e4, white's second move then, the move for which the QG gets its name, is pawn to c4. This image displays what the position will look like at this point, with black to move.
